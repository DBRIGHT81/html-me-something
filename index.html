Sega Nomad

In 1995, Sega attempted to re-enter the handheld gaming market a second time with the Nomad. Basically a smaller version of the Sega Genesis, it was released late in it's parent's life cycle & doomed it to fail for Sega putting all of their focus on the just released Sega 32X and Sega Saturn. Luckily for me, I was able to grab one from eBay for a third of the original price. It has an extra port that lets you hook up another controller so you can play with a friend. The plus side is having the ability to play almost every Sega Genesis title on the go. Just make sure you have six AA batteries at your disposal.

NeoGeo Pocket Color

The NeoGeo Pocket Color was the last system produced by Japanese gaming company SNK. It would have a relatively short lifespan of eighteen months(May 1999-October 2000), due to financial woes of the company and being obliterated by its handheld competitor in Nintendo with their Pokenon francise. Despite its commercial failings, it's(in my opinion) one of the best handheld systems ever created. One of the main innovations of the NeoGeo Pocket Color was its joystick instead of the traditional directional pad. Its short and reccessed made controlling every game feel effortless, no matter the game you were playing. This one won't break the bank on battteries since it only takes two AAs and are pretty easy to aquire due to not being a high priority on most collectors list.

Retrobit Super Retro Advance Adapter

The Super Retro Advance by Retrobit is a neat little device that allows you to play GameBoy Advance games on your Super Nintendo. This thing is a life saver if you ever had issues seeing the small visuals on the original GBA screen, now giving the user the ability to play on larger TVs. No battery power needed here since it uses the same power of the Super Nintendo. The Super Retro Advance used to go for around $40USD, but is now out of production and the price on the collector's market has skyrocketed to $250.
